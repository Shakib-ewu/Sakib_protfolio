Our undergraduate thesis examined mental health among physically challenged individuals versus the general population in Bangladesh during the COVID-19 pandemic. Using data from 22 physically challenged and 71 general participants, we found that interpersonal support positively correlates with well-being in both groups, while age negatively impacts well-being among general participants. A Random Forest–based machine learning model achieved over 93% accuracy in classifying the groups. The findings highlight distinct mental health factors and the need for tailored support strategies.
